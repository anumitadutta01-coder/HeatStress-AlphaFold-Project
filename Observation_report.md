##FINAL COMPARATIVE INTERPRETATION — RICE vs WHEAT vs MAIZE

2. Rice (Q657C0) shows the most organised structure dominant alpha helices with moderate disorder suggesting a more defined and stable heat stress response mechanism.

5. Despite these structural differences, all three proteins  share the same fundamental design  alpha helices for DNA binding and disordered loops for flexible interactions confirming conserved heat stress response mechanisms across cereal crops with species-specific structural adaptations.

##CONCLUSION –

This comparative structural analysis reveals that while heat stress transcription factors are evolutionarily conserved  across rice, wheat and maize, each species shows unique  structural adaptations possibly reflecting differences in  their natural environments and heat stress tolerance  mechanisms.
